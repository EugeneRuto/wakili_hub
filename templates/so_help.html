Sexual Offense Help Guide

If you're facing a sexual offense issue, you're not alone. Below is a comprehensive guide to help you navigate the legal system and access the resources available to you in Kenya.

How to Find Legal Assistance

1. Report to the Police

Start by reporting the incident to your nearest police station. Ensure you obtain an Occurrence Book (OB) number after recording your statement.

2. Seek Medical Attention

Visit a healthcare facility immediately for a medical check-up and obtain a P3 Form. The P3 Form is essential for legal proceedings in Kenya.

Steps to Take and Resources Available

3. File a Case

With assistance from legal professionals or paralegal support, file your case at the appropriate court. You can also approach NGOs specializing in gender-based violence for help.

4. Counseling Services

Seek counseling from accredited organizations such as:

  • Gender Violence Recovery Centre (GVRC)
  • FIDA Kenya
  • Healthcare facilities with counseling services

5. Community Support

Engage with community-based organizations or helplines such as the Childline Kenya 116 toll-free number for support and guidance.
