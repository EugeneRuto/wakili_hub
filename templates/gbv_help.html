Legal Help for Gender-Based Violence Victims in Kenya

Comprehensive guidance to help victims of gender-based violence seek justice and navigate the Kenyan legal system.

Steps to Take When Facing Gender-Based Violence

  1. Step 1: Ensure Your Safety

    If you are in immediate danger, seek safety first. Find a secure location, such as a relative's house, a trusted friend's home, or a shelter.

  2. Step 2: Report the Incident

    Report the violence to the nearest police station or call the emergency hotline 999 or the GBV helpline 1195. Ensure that the police record the case in the Occurrence Book (OB) and provide you with an OB number for reference.

  3. Step 3: Seek Medical Assistance

    Visit a hospital or a Gender-Based Violence Recovery Centre for medical treatment and documentation of injuries. A Post Rape Care (PRC) Form will be issued, which is critical for legal proceedings.

  4. Step 4: File a Protection Order

    Under the Protection Against Domestic Violence Act, 2015, you can apply for a protection order at the Magistrates' Court to safeguard yourself from further harm.

  5. Step 5: Access Legal Support

    Contact legal aid organizations such as FIDA Kenya or the National Legal Aid Service (NLAS) for free legal assistance.

Legal Process for Gender-Based Violence Cases

The Kenyan legal system has specific provisions to handle GBV cases. Follow these stages:

Stage 1: Filing a Report

File a formal report at the police station. Provide as much evidence as possible, including medical reports and witness statements.

Stage 2: Investigation

The police will investigate the case and gather evidence to support prosecution.

Stage 3: Prosecution

The Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions (ODPP) handles the case in court. Victims may be required to testify as witnesses.

Stage 4: Court Verdict

The court determines the outcome, including penalties for the perpetrator. Victims may also seek damages for harm caused.
